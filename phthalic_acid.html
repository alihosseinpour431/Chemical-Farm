Hazard Classification

Irritant: skin/eye irritation; respiratory irritation (dust)

Phthalic acid is a white crystalline aromatic diacid used mainly as an intermediate in the phthalic anhydride (PA) value chain and in resin/plasticizer chemistry. Dust may irritate the respiratory tract; contact may irritate skin and eyes. Use goggles, gloves, and local exhaust ventilation where dust can form; avoid generating airborne dust during charging, milling, or bag handling.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may worsen irritation; dermatitis risk with frequent contact

Repeated exposure to dust in powder handling can lead to persistent respiratory irritation (cough/sore throat) and recurring eye irritation. Frequent skin contact may cause dryness and dermatitis. Prefer closed transfer, dust-tight packaging/feeding, and housekeeping (vacuuming vs sweeping). Apply occupational exposure controls during reactor charging and dryer discharge.

Production Process & Capacity

Phthalic acid is commonly handled within the phthalic anhydride (PA) value chain via oxidation of o-xylene followed by hydration / finishing steps (depending on product slate).
1 Feedstock Preparation
o-Xylene feed and air are conditioned; impurity and moisture control supports stable catalyst performance.
2 Catalytic Oxidation
Vapor-phase oxidation converts o-xylene to phthalic anhydride over vanadium-based catalysts; temperature control limits by-products.
3 Hydration & Purification
Depending on product slate, PA can be hydrated to phthalic acid and purified (filtration/crystallization) to meet resin/ester specs.
4 Packaging & Logistics
Handled as a solid; packaging (bags/big-bags) and dust control are critical in loading/unloading and storage areas.
