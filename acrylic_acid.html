Hazard Classification

Corrosive, flammable; can polymerize violently if uninhibited/heated

Transport is commonly regulated as UN 2218 (ACRYLIC ACID, STABILIZED), Class 8 with subsidiary risk 3 in many schemes. Keep inhibitor within spec, control temperature, avoid contamination (peroxides, rust, metals), and use corrosion-compatible equipment with ventilation and emergency shower/eyewash.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may cause dermatitis and respiratory irritation; mists/vapors should be controlled

Use local exhaust ventilation at charging/loading points, acid-resistant gloves, goggles/face shield, and splash protection. Avoid confined-space entry without monitoring and rescue plan. Maintain inhibitor management and temperature controls to reduce runaway polymerization risk.

Production Process & Capacity

Main route: two-step catalytic oxidation of propylene (via acrolein) , followed by absorption, purification, and stabilized storage to prevent polymerization.
1Feed Preparation & Safety Dilution
Propylene is mixed with air/steam; steam moderates hotspots and improves selectivity in fixed-bed tubular reactors.
2Step 1 Oxidation: Propylene → Acrolein
Catalytic partial oxidation forms acrolein (and some acrylic acid); tight temperature control limits deep oxidation to COx.
3Step 2 Oxidation + Absorption
Acrolein is further oxidized to acrylic acid; product is captured via quench/absorption, then routed to purification.
4Purification, Stabilization & Logistics
Distillation delivers glacial/technical specs; inhibitor dosing (MEHQ/HQ), temperature control, and corrosion-compatible storage are critical.
