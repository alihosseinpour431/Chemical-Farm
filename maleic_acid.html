Hazard Classification

Corrosive/irritant; respiratory irritation; may cause allergic skin reaction

Maleic acid is the cis-isomer of butenedioic acid and a reactive dicarboxylic acid used mainly as an intermediate (notably to make fumaric acid) and in resin/additive routes. Dust or contact can cause severe eye damage and skin burns/irritation, and inhalation of dust/mist may irritate the respiratory tract. Use chemical goggles/face shield, acid-resistant gloves, protective clothing, and local exhaust ventilation during charging and bag handling.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may worsen dermatitis/eye irritation; sensitization possible

Chronic risk is typically dominated by repeated acid contact and dust exposure: persistent eye/airway irritation and dermatitis can occur, and some SDS indicate skin sensitization potential. Favor closed transfer, dust-tight feeding, and wet methods for cleanup (avoid dry sweeping). Implement exposure monitoring and training in resin/additive and intermediate production plants.

Production Process & Capacity

Maleic acid is typically produced by hydrolysis (hydration) of maleic anhydride followed by crystallization and drying; it can be further converted to fumaric acid and other derivatives.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Maleic anhydride feed (often from n-butane oxidation value chains) is conditioned; water quality and impurity control support product color and stability.
2 Hydrolysis / Hydration Reaction
Maleic anhydride reacts with water to form maleic acid in controlled aqueous conditions; temperature and addition rate limit hotspots and side reactions.
3 Crystallization & Purification
Cooling and crystallization recover maleic acid; filtration and washing reduce impurities (color/odor) to meet resin/intermediate specifications.
4 Drying & Logistics
Dried crystals/granules are packed in bags or big-bags; dust control and corrosion-aware materials of construction improve safe handling.
