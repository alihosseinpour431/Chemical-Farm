Hazard Classification

Toxicity is primarily via ingestion; systemic effects possible

MEG is widely used industrially but is dangerous if swallowed. Ingestion can lead to metabolic acidosis and kidney injury. Keep away from food/drinks, prevent access by children/pets, and store in clearly labeled containers. Avoid aerosols/mists; use gloves and eye protection during handling.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated contact may irritate skin/eyes; prevent ingestion at all costs

MEG has low volatility at ambient conditions, yet splashes and contaminated hands are common exposure routes. Wash hands after handling, avoid eating/drinking in work areas, and ensure spill cleanup procedures. Where heated systems are used (heat-transfer fluids), provide ventilation and minimize mist formation.

Production Process & Capacity

MEG is primarily produced from ethylene oxide (EO) hydration / hydrolysis , yielding MEG alongside heavier glycols (DEG/TEG). In China, an additional route is coal-to-MEG via oxalate/hydrogenation (CTEG) .
1 Feedstock & Purification
Ethylene/oxygen feeds are conditioned for EO production; water quality and impurity control are critical for glycol selectivity and corrosion management.
2 EO Production & Conversion
Ethylene is oxidized to EO, then EO is hydrated to glycols. Advanced schemes (e.g., carbonate/OMEGA-type) aim to maximize MEG selectivity and reduce DEG/TEG.
3 Separation & Fractionation
Water removal and distillation isolate MEG and heavier glycols. Energy integration and vacuum systems strongly affect OPEX and achievable purity.
4 Product Polishing & Logistics
Final polishing targets PET-grade specifications (color, acidity, aldehydes, ions). Logistics are global; China-centric flows often track polyester operating rates and import arbitrage.

Global Demand Analysis

MEG demand is dominated by PET/polyester chains and therefore is highly sensitive to packaging, textile fibers, and operating rates of PTA/PET plants. Asia-Pacific leads global consumption, with China acting as a demand-and-price gravity center.
