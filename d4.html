Hazard Classification

Irritant to skin/eyes/respiratory system; combustible solid; harmful to aquatic life

Octadecylamine is a long-chain primary amine. Dust may cause irritation to eyes, skin, and respiratory tract. Avoid dust generation; use local exhaust ventilation during handling. Wear appropriate PPE (gloves, goggles, dust mask). Store in cool, dry conditions away from oxidizers. Environmental release must be prevented due to aquatic toxicity.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may cause sensitization; potential respiratory sensitization

Repeated or prolonged skin contact may lead to dermatitis. Some long-chain amines have shown potential for respiratory sensitization in occupational settings. Implement engineering controls to minimize dust exposure, conduct air monitoring where appropriate, and follow good industrial hygiene practices.

Production Process & Capacity

Octadecylamine is primarily produced via catalytic amination of stearyl alcohol (C18 alcohol) or hydrogenation of stearyl nitrile , followed by purification to remove secondary/tertiary amine by-products.
1Feedstock Preparation
Stearyl alcohol (C18) or stearyl nitrile is purified and dried; ammonia (for amination) or hydrogen (for nitrile route) is conditioned to catalyst specifications.
2Catalytic Reaction
Amination: C18OH + NH3 → C18NH2 + H2O over Ni/Cu catalysts at 150–200°C and 100–300 bar. Nitrile route: C17H35CN + 2H2 → C18H39N over Ni catalysts; temperature/pressure control minimizes secondary amine formation.
3Separation & Purification
Unreacted ammonia/hydrogen is recycled; crude product is distilled under vacuum to separate primary amine (target) from secondary/tertiary amines and heavies. High-purity grades (>95%) require precise fractionation control.
4Solidification & Packaging
Molten amine is flaked or prilled and packaged in moisture-resistant bags/containers. Storage under nitrogen blanket prevents oxidation/yellowing. Dust control during handling is essential for operator safety and product quality.

Global Demand Analysis

Demand is driven by filming amine corrosion inhibitors for steam/water systems in power generation (fossil & nuclear), refining, and industrial facilities. Asia-Pacific leads consumption due to coal-fired power expansion and refinery growth; North America and Europe maintain steady demand for asset integrity.

Corrosion mechanism: Octadecylamine's long hydrophobic tail forms a protective monolayer film on carbon steel surfaces in steam condensate, neutralizing CO₂ acidity and blocking oxygen/water contact—reducing flow-accelerated corrosion (FAC) by >80% in well-dosed systems.
