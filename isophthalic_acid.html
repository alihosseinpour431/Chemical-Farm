Hazard Classification

Dust irritant; avoid inhalation; eye/skin irritation possible

Isophthalic acid (IPA/PIA) is a high-melting aromatic diacid used as a comonomer in polyester value chains. It is typically handled as a crystalline solid; dust can irritate eyes and the respiratory tract. Use goggles, gloves, and local ventilation during bag dumping, conveying, and drying; minimize dust formation and clean with vacuum systems suitable for fine powders.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may sustain irritation

Repeated exposure to fine dust may contribute to persistent throat/nasal irritation and dryness; prolonged skin contact can cause redness or dryness in sensitive individuals. Favor sealed transfer, bag filters with proper maintenance, and good housekeeping to prevent dust accumulation.

Production Process & Capacity

IPA is typically produced by liquid-phase oxidation of m-xylene followed by crystallization and purification to PIA grades.
1 Feedstock Preparation
m-Xylene feed is dried/filtered; solvent (often acetic acid) and catalyst system are prepared for stable oxidation.
2 Catalytic Oxidation
Air oxidation converts m-xylene to isophthalic acid; temperature, oxygen transfer, and catalyst control limit by-products.
3 Crystallization & Purification
Solid IPA is crystallized, filtered, and washed; purification steps target color, metal residues, and polymer-grade specifications (PIA).
4 Drying, Storage & Logistics
Product is dried and packed (bags/big-bags/bulk); dust control and quality preservation are critical for polymer and coating customers.
