Hazard Classification

Dust control: nuisance dust, avoid inhalation; keep away from ignition sources

Zinc stearate is typically handled as a fine hydrophobic powder. Use local exhaust ventilation, minimize airborne dust during transfer, and keep good housekeeping to reduce combustible dust risk. Use goggles and a dust mask/respirator where needed.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may irritate airways; skin dryness possible

Long-term exposure to airborne particulates can contribute to respiratory irritation in sensitive individuals. Maintain dust controls, use PPE as required, and follow SDS/occupational exposure guidance for particulate limits.

Production Process & Capacity

Zinc stearate is commonly produced by neutralization/precipitation of stearic acid with zinc oxide or zinc salts , followed by filtration, drying, and micronization to match PVC/rubber specs.
1Feedstock Preparation
Stearic acid (or fatty acid blend) is melted and conditioned; zinc oxide (or zinc salts) is prepared and metered. Water/solvent phase and additives may be prepared to control particle size.
2Reaction & Salt Formation
Under controlled temperature and agitation, zinc reacts with fatty acid to form zinc stearate. pH/stoichiometry control limits free acid and improves consistency.
3Separation & Drying
The solid product is filtered/centrifuged and dried. Moisture control is critical for PVC processing performance and storage stability.
4Milling, Classification & Packaging
Micronization and classification tune particle size distribution (PSD) for mold-release/lubrication behavior. Dust-safe packaging and handling are implemented.
