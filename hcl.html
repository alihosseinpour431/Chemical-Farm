Hazard Classification

Corrosive; toxic if inhaled; severe eye/skin damage

Hydrochloric acid solutions release hydrogen chloride vapors that can severely irritate and damage the respiratory tract. It is strongly corrosive to skin and eyes. Use chemical goggles/face shield, acid-resistant gloves, and local exhaust ventilation—especially during unloading, dilution, or cleaning in confined spaces.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may cause chronic respiratory irritation and dental erosion

Chronic or repeated exposure to acidic mists/vapors can contribute to persistent airway irritation and, in some occupational settings, dental erosion. Favor closed transfer, avoid mist formation, apply scrubbers/ventilation, and implement exposure monitoring for acid mists where relevant.

Production Process & Capacity

HCl is commonly supplied from direct synthesis (H2 + Cl2) or byproduct recovery (chlorination/phosgenation) followed by absorption of HCl gas into water.
1 Feed & Utilities
Condition chlorine and hydrogen (or HCl-rich offgas) and ensure drying/purity and safe ratio control.
2 Reaction / Capture
Produce HCl gas via controlled burner synthesis, or capture HCl from chlorination/phosgenation units.
3 Absorption & Strength Control
Absorb HCl gas into demineralized water; control concentration (e.g., 30–35%) and heat removal.
4 Storage & Logistics
Store in corrosion-resistant tanks; ship in bulk/IBC with fume control and compatible materials.
