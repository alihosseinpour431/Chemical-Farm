Hazard Classification

Combustible dust risk; avoid dust clouds and ignition sources

Handle as a solid with dust control: local exhaust ventilation, housekeeping, and grounded transfer where dust may accumulate. Keep away from strong oxidizers. Use goggles and gloves; avoid creating airborne dust.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may irritate eyes/airways; maintain hygiene and ventilation

Repeated contact can cause irritation; wash exposed skin and avoid rubbing eyes. For dusty operations, use dust masks/respiratory protection as required by site risk assessment.

Transport note: some supplier SDS list adipic acid as not regulated for transport (ADR/IMDG/IATA). Always verify the SDS for your specific grade and packaging.

Production Process & Capacity

Industrial adipic acid is primarily produced via nitric-acid oxidation of cyclohexane/cyclohexanone/cyclohexanol . bio-based routes are expanding for footprint reduction.
1Feedstock & KA Oil Preparation
Cyclohexane is oxidized to a cyclohexanol/cyclohexanone mixture (KA oil), or cyclohexanone/cyclohexanol streams are conditioned for oxidation.
2Nitric-Acid Oxidation
KA oil is oxidized using nitric acid to form adipic acid. Heat removal and controlled reaction conditions are critical for selectivity and safety.
3Crystallization & Purification
Adipic acid is crystallized, filtered, washed, and dried to meet polymer-grade specifications. Mother-liquor handling affects yield and wastewater load.
4Solid Logistics (Bags / Big-Bags / Bulk)
Product is shipped as solid powder/flakes/prills. Dust control, dry storage, and contamination prevention are key for polymer-grade supply chains.
