Hazard Classification

Dust/vapors irritate eyes/airways; sensitization risk; avoid dust clouds

Phthalic anhydride is a solid that can form irritating dust and may cause respiratory sensitization with repeated exposure. Use local exhaust ventilation, dust-tight transfer, and proper PPE (goggles, gloves, and suitable respiratory protection when needed). Keep away from ignition sources and prevent dust accumulation; in large-scale handling, dust-cloud ignition and hot-surface decomposition are key risks.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may trigger asthma-like symptoms; focus on dust control

Chronic exposure to PA dust can lead to persistent eye/skin irritation and respiratory sensitization. Industrial best practice is closed conveying, bag filters, housekeeping to avoid dust buildup, and medical surveillance in high-exposure jobs. China’s large plasticizer and resin clusters add scale pressure—so EHS and emissions control (VOC/CO off-gas treatment) are emphasized in modern plants.

Production Process & Capacity

PA is mainly produced by gas-phase catalytic oxidation of o-xylene (modern route); separation yields flakes/powder. China’s scale advantage often comes from integrated chemical parks near plasticizer & resin demand hubs.
1Feedstock & Air Preparation
o-Xylene is purified and vaporized; air/oxygen is conditioned. Tight control prevents hot spots and improves selectivity.
2Catalytic Oxidation Reactor
Gas-phase oxidation occurs over vanadium-based catalysts. Heat removal and reactor design are critical for stable operation.
3Product Recovery & Purification
PA is recovered by cooling/condensation and purified to meet plasticizer/resin specs; product is typically flaked or milled.
4Off-gas Treatment & Safety
Off-gas may contain VOC/CO and is treated by catalytic oxidation/abatement. Dust-tight handling and housekeeping reduce combustible-dust risk.

Modern Chinese units increasingly invest in off-gas treatment and plant EHS upgrades.
