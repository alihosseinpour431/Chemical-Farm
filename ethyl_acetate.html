Hazard Classification

Highly flammable liquid and vapor; vapor travel & ignition risk

Ethyl acetate is a volatile solvent with low flash point. Keep away from heat/sparks/open flames; use explosion-proof ventilation where needed. Vapors are heavier than air and may travel to an ignition source. Ground/bond containers during transfer to reduce static ignition risk.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may irritate eyes/airways; may cause CNS effects at high vapor levels

Repeated contact can cause skin defatting and dryness. High vapor concentrations may cause headache, dizziness, or drowsiness. Minimize inhalation exposure, avoid mist formation, and use gloves/goggles. Ensure good ventilation, especially in printing/coating operations.

Production Process & Capacity

Ethyl acetate is typically produced by esterification of ethanol with acetic acid (often with reactive distillation), then purified to commercial/urethane grades for solvent and extraction uses.
1Feedstock Preparation
Ethanol and acetic acid are dried/conditioned; water control is critical because esterification equilibrium is water-sensitive.
2Catalytic Reaction
In acid-catalyzed esterification, ethanol reacts with acetic acid to form ethyl acetate and water; conversion is boosted by removing products.
3Reactive Distillation / Separation
Reactive distillation can shift equilibrium by separating light components and managing water; azeotrope handling and reflux strategy matter.
4Purification & Storage
Final polishing targets water/acid traces for solvent-grade and high-purity grades; store as a flammable solvent with vapor control and grounding.
