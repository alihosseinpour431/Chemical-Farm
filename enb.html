Hazard Classification

Flammable liquid/vapor; manage ignition sources and static

ENB is a volatile diene used as the third monomer in EPDM. Keep away from heat/sparks/open flames, use explosion-proof ventilation where needed, and ground/bond containers during transfer to reduce static ignition risk.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may irritate; skin defatting/dryness possible

Repeated skin contact may cause dryness/dermatitis. Vapors can irritate eyes/airways; minimize inhalation, avoid aerosol formation, and use gloves/goggles.

Production Process & Capacity

ENB is commonly produced via Diels–Alder to vinyl-norbornene followed by isomerization , then purified by distillation and stabilized with inhibitor.
1Feedstock & C5/C4 Preparation
Cyclopentadiene (often from DCPD cracking) and butadiene streams are prepared and dried; inhibitor strategy is managed to avoid runaway polymerization.
2Diels–Alder (VNB Formation)
Butadiene reacts with cyclopentadiene to form vinyl-norbornene (VNB). Heat removal and selectivity control are key for yield and safety.
3Isomerization to ENB
VNB is isomerized to ethylidene-norbornene (ENB). Catalyst choice and residence time tune conversion while minimizing side reactions.
4Fractionation, Inhibition & Logistics
Distillation achieves EPDM-grade purity; product is stabilized (commonly with BHT) and stored/handled as a flammable liquid with vapor control and grounding/bonding.
