Hazard Classification

Corrosive; causes severe skin burns/eye damage; flammable liquid (concentrated); harmful if inhaled

Acetic acid (especially glacial grades) is corrosive and can cause severe burns. Vapors/mists irritate the respiratory tract; heated handling increases vapor exposure. Keep away from ignition sources when handling concentrated material, use corrosion-resistant equipment, local exhaust ventilation, and full PPE (goggles/face shield, acid-resistant gloves, protective clothing).

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure may cause dermatitis, tooth erosion, and chronic irritation (vapors/mists)

Prolonged skin contact can lead to dermatitis; repeated inhalation of vapors/mists may contribute to ongoing respiratory irritation. In workplaces with heated acetic acid or mist-generating operations, prioritize closed transfer, scrubber/ventilation controls, and exposure monitoring to keep airborne concentrations low.

Production Process & Capacity

Industrial acetic acid is primarily produced via methanol carbonylation (Cativa/Monsanto family) followed by separation, drying, and grade finishing (glacial or aqueous).
1 Feedstock Preparation
Purified methanol and carbon monoxide are dried/conditioned; iodide promoters and catalyst system are managed for stable selectivity.
2 Carbonylation Reaction
Methanol is carbonylated to acetic acid under homogeneous catalysis; water balance and catalyst stability drive yield and energy use.
3 Separation & Purification
Flashing/distillation recover catalyst/iodides and remove water/light ends; final columns meet glacial purity and color specifications.
4 Storage, Corrosion & Logistics
Stored in compatible materials (corrosion control); shipped in bulk/IBC/drums; manage fumes and ignition sources for concentrated grades.
