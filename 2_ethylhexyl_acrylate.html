Hazard Classification

Flammable liquid (Category 3) | Skin sensitizer (Category 1) | Suspected reproductive toxin (Category 2, EU CLP) | Polymerization hazard if inhibitor depleted

2-EHA is a flammable liquid requiring storage away from heat/ignition sources. It is a potent skin sensitizer—repeated exposure may cause allergic contact dermatitis. EU CLP classifies it as suspected of damaging fertility or the unborn child (H361d). Always contains polymerization inhibitors (HQ, MEHQ ~10–50 ppm); NEVER use if inhibitor level falls below specification. Use only with engineering controls (closed transfer, ventilation), PPE (nitrile gloves, goggles, respirator for vapor), and strict temperature control during handling/storage.

Chronic Health & Handling Protocols

Sensitization is irreversible; strict exposure control mandatory. Reproductive toxicity concerns require special handling for pregnant workers.

Once sensitized to acrylates, individuals typically remain allergic for life. Implement rigorous exposure control: closed transfer systems, local exhaust ventilation at dispensing points, mandatory air monitoring, and medical surveillance programs. Inhibitor levels must be tested quarterly; material rejected if <8 ppm MEHQ. Storage temperature maintained <25°C with nitrogen padding to prevent peroxide formation. EU REACH requires strict workplace exposure limits (WELs) and substitution assessments where technically feasible.

Production Process & Capacity

2-EHA is produced via acid-catalyzed esterification of acrylic acid with 2-ethylhexanol , followed by purification to remove water, unreacted feedstocks, and heavies. Inhibitors (MEHQ) added before storage.
1Feedstock Preparation
Glacial acrylic acid (≥99%) and 2-ethylhexanol (≥99.5%) are dried and preheated; sulfuric acid or p-toluenesulfonic acid catalyst (0.1–0.5 wt%) is charged to reactor. Inhibitor (MEHQ 50–100 ppm) present throughout to prevent premature polymerization.
2Esterification Reaction
CH₂=CHCOOH + C₈H₁₇OH ⇌ CH₂=CHCOOC₈H₁₇ + H₂O at 110–140°C. Water by-product continuously removed via azeotropic distillation (often with toluene/xylene entrainer) to drive equilibrium toward ester formation. Reaction conversion >98% targeted.
3Neutralization & Purification
Crude product neutralized with Na₂CO₃/NaOH to remove residual acid catalyst; washed with water to remove salts. Purified via vacuum distillation (bp ~105–110°C @ 20 mmHg) to separate 2-EHA from unreacted alcohol, acrylic acid, and di-ester heavies. Final product clarity and color (APHA <20) verified.
4Inhibition & Storage
MEHQ inhibitor adjusted to 10–50 ppm; oxygen (air) maintained in headspace to regenerate inhibitor. Stored in carbon steel or stainless tanks under nitrogen blanket at <25°C. Inhibitor levels monitored quarterly; material discarded if polymerization onset temperature drops below 80°C (DSC test).

Global Demand Analysis

Demand driven by pressure-sensitive adhesive growth in e-commerce packaging, automotive assembly tapes, and medical wearable devices. Asia-Pacific leads consumption (48%) due to electronics manufacturing and packaging expansion; North America maintains strong demand for industrial tapes and labels. Europe focuses on medical-grade and sustainable PSA formulations.

Critical safety note: 2-EHA polymerization incidents have caused fatalities. NEVER distill to dryness. ALWAYS verify inhibitor levels before heating. Use emergency cooling/quench systems on reactors. Thermal stability testing (DSC/ARC) mandatory for process design.
