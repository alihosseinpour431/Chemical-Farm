Hazard Classification

Irritant; causes serious eye damage; dust may irritate respiratory tract

Glutaric acid (pentanedioic acid) is a C5 aliphatic diacid used as a chemical intermediate in resins, plasticizers, polyol/polyester routes, and specialty synthesis. It is typically handled as a solid; avoid dust generation. Eye contact with dust or solutions can cause serious damage—use goggles/face shield and good hygiene. Provide local exhaust ventilation during charging, milling, or bag dumping.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may cause irritation; prolonged contact may dry/irritate skin

In routine industrial handling, the main chronic concerns are repeated irritation from dust or acidic solutions and dermatitis from prolonged contact. Keep transfer systems closed where possible, use dust collection for bag handling, and implement PPE and housekeeping (avoid dry sweeping; use vacuum systems rated for dust).

Production Process & Capacity

Glutaric acid is commonly sourced from adipic-acid chain side streams (AGS/DBA recovery) and purified for resin/plasticizer/intermediate markets.
1 Feed/Stream Preparation
Mother liquor/diacid mixtures from adipic value chain are conditioned; acidity, metals and water are managed for stable separation.
2 Fractionation & Separation
Crystallization/neutralization-fractionation separates C4–C6 diacids; selectivity and impurity control limit color/odor issues.
3 Purification & Finishing
Filtration, washing, and drying meet specs for moisture, ash/metals, and color; milling controls particle size for downstream use.
4 Storage & Logistics
Packed in bags/big-bags; dust control is critical during bagging and unloading. Keep dry and avoid contact with incompatible bases.
