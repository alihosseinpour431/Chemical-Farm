Hazard Classification

Reproductive hazard (H360D); skin/eye irritant (H315/H319); may irritate airways (H335)

NMP is classified in many jurisdictions as a reproductive toxicant (may damage the unborn child). Use closed handling where feasible, minimize inhalation/dermal exposure, and apply strict PPE (gloves, goggles, protective clothing). Pay special attention to exposure control in battery/electronics plants where NMP is used at scale (notably in China’s LIB hubs).

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure increases risk; focus on dermal control and ventilation

Repeated exposure can lead to irritation/dermatitis and increases reproductive/developmental risk. Implement exposure monitoring (DNEL-based programs where required), engineering controls, and solvent recovery to reduce workplace concentrations—widely practiced in large-scale Asian (incl. China) battery manufacturing.

Production Process & Capacity

NMP is typically produced from gamma-butyrolactone (GBL) + methylamine followed by purification. In China’s battery manufacturing hubs, closed-loop recovery/recycling is widely used to reduce emissions and solvent cost.
1Feedstock Preparation
GBL is dried/purified; methylamine supply and inert handling are prepared for stable reaction and consistent product quality.
2Amidation & Cyclization
GBL reacts with methylamine to form NMP (lactam formation). Reaction conditions are controlled to limit by-products and color.
3Distillation & Polishing
Vacuum distillation and polishing remove water/impurities to reach battery/electronics-grade specs—critical for China’s LIB supply chain.
4Quality Control & Storage
Moisture and trace metals are controlled for sensitive applications. Store in compatible containers; implement exposure controls and vapor management.

Global Demand Analysis

NMP demand is anchored in electronics and lithium-ion battery manufacturing (PVDF binder solvent) plus petrochemical extraction and pharma synthesis. Asia-Pacific leads the market; China plays a central role as a major consumer and increasingly as a producer/recycler due to scale and VOC/EHS compliance.
