Hazard Classification

Dust/particulate irritant; combustible dust hazard; incompatible with strong oxidizers

Terephthalic acid (PTA/TPA) is a low-volatility solid used at large scale in polyester value chains. While it is not a highly flammable liquid, fine dust can form a combustible dust cloud in air under unfavorable conditions. Avoid dust accumulation, use local exhaust ventilation at bagging/handling points, and keep away from strong oxidizers. Use eye protection and a suitable particulate respirator where dust is generated.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may irritate eyes/airways; good hygiene and controls recommended

Long-term risk is primarily linked to repeated particulate exposure during unloading, conveying, drying, and bagging. Use closed transfer where feasible, maintain housekeeping to prevent dust layers, and apply PPE (goggles, gloves, and particulate respirator) according to workplace risk assessment.

Production Process & Capacity

PTA is typically produced by aerobic oxidation of p-xylene (PX) to crude terephthalic acid (CTA), followed by purification (hydrogenation/crystallization) to polymer-grade PTA.
1 Feedstock & Catalyst System
Paraxylene (PX) is fed to an oxidation reactor with a Co/Mn/Br catalyst system; acetic acid is commonly used as solvent in the oxidation step.
2 Aerobic Oxidation (CTA Formation)
Air/oxygen oxidizes PX to crude terephthalic acid (CTA). Key impurity control targets include 4-carboxybenzaldehyde (4-CBA) and related aromatics.
3 Purification to Polymer-Grade PTA
CTA is purified (often via hydrogenation) and recrystallized/filtered to reduce 4-CBA to polymer-grade specifications; drying yields crystalline PTA.
4 Storage, Handling & Logistics
Handled as a powder: enclosed conveying, dust collection, and housekeeping reduce exposure and combustible dust risk; shipped in bulk bags/silos.
