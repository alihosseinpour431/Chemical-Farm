Hazard Classification

Irritant; serious eye damage risk; dust may irritate respiratory tract

Benzoic acid is an aromatic carboxylic acid used widely as a preservative intermediate (benzoates) and as a building block for resins, plasticizers, and benzoyl derivatives. Handle solids to minimize dust; avoid eye contact; use goggles and gloves. Provide local exhaust during charging, drying, or milling to reduce inhalation exposure.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure risk (systemic effects possible)

Repeated or prolonged exposure to dust may sustain irritation and, in occupational settings, warrants exposure controls (enclosed handling, dust collection, hygiene). Follow SDS guidance and applicable OEL/controls for your jurisdiction and task.

Production Process & Capacity

Benzoic acid is typically produced by catalytic air oxidation of toluene followed by crystallization, filtration, and drying.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Toluene feed is conditioned; catalysts/solvent system and oxygen/air supply are prepared with controls for temperature and off-gas.
2 Oxidation Reaction
Liquid-phase oxidation converts toluene to benzoic acid; selectivity is managed via catalyst choice (often Co/Mn systems) and temperature control.
3 Crystallization & Solid Handling
Crystallization separates benzoic acid; filtration/centrifugation and washing improve purity; drying sets moisture and particle specs.
4 Storage & Packaging
Packed as flakes/powder (bags/big-bags); dust control and product hygiene are key for food/feed and high-purity grades.
