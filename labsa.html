Hazard Classification

Corrosive; causes severe skin burns and eye damage; harmful if swallowed

LABSA is a strong organic sulfonic acid used as the key intermediate for producing LAS (linear alkylbenzene sulfonate) detergents. Industrial grades are highly acidic and can aggressively irritate or burn skin/eyes. Avoid aerosols/mists, use chemical-resistant gloves, face shield/goggles, and local exhaust ventilation—especially during transfer, neutralization, or spill cleanup.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated contact may cause dermatitis; mists may irritate respiratory tract

Repeated skin exposure can lead to persistent irritation and dermatitis; repeated eye/face splash risk is significant in handling corrosive liquids. Maintain closed transfer where possible, implement eyewash/shower stations, and control neutralization heat and splashing (slow addition, mixing control).

Production Process & Capacity

LABSA is typically produced by continuous sulfonation of LAB with SO3 (SO3/air) in film (falling-film) reactors, followed by digestion/finishing; it is then neutralized to LAS for detergents.
1 SO3 Generation & Dry Air
Dry air is prepared; SO3 is generated (commonly via sulfur burning + catalytic conversion) and conditioned to the target SO3-in-air concentration.
2 Film Sulfonation (LAB + SO3)
LAB reacts with SO3 in a multitube falling-film reactor; heat removal and SO3 ratio control drive color and sulfonation degree.
3 Digestion / Finishing
Aging/digestion and controlled water management reduce residual SO3 and stabilize product; filtration/degassing improves quality.
4 Storage & Safe Handling
Stored as a corrosive liquid in compatible materials; pumps/lines require corrosion control and splash protection; spills are neutralized carefully.
