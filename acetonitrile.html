Hazard Classification

Highly flammable liquid/vapor; ignition + static discharge risk

Acetonitrile (MeCN) has a low flash point and forms flammable vapors. Keep away from heat/sparks/open flames, use explosion-proof ventilation where needed, and ground/bond containers during transfer to reduce static ignition risk.

Chronic Health Effects

May cause skin dryness; high vapor exposure can be dangerous

Repeated skin contact can cause defatting and dermatitis. Inhalation of high vapor concentrations may cause CNS symptoms; acetonitrile can be metabolized to cyanide in the body—minimize inhalation exposure, avoid mist formation, and use gloves/goggles.

Production Process & Capacity

Acetonitrile is mainly obtained as a co-product of acrylonitrile (propylene ammoxidation / Sohio) , then purified by distillation. China is a major consumer and one of the key supply hubs due to its large integrated petrochemical base.
1 Feed & Reactor Section
Propylene, ammonia, and air react over catalysts to make acrylonitrile; acetonitrile forms as a co-product in the reaction network.
2 Quench, Absorption & Crude Separation
Hot reactor effluent is quenched and absorbed; crude nitrile streams are routed to separation where acetonitrile is recovered alongside other nitriles.
3 Distillation & Spec Control
Fractionation isolates acetonitrile from the nitrile slate. Tight cut control and water management are key for solvent/analytical grades.
4 Storage, Vapor Control & Logistics
Because MeCN is flammable and volatile, facilities use explosion-proof design, vapor recovery, and strict grounding/bonding. China’s large electronics/pharma clusters drive strong demand for high-purity logistics.
