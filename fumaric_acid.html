Hazard Classification

Eye irritant (GHS); dust may irritate respiratory tract

Fumaric acid is typically classified as an eye irritant (H319). Handle powders to minimize dust generation; use safety goggles, gloves, and local exhaust ventilation during charging, milling, or bag-dumping. In case of airborne dust, use appropriate respiratory protection and good housekeeping to prevent dust accumulation.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may cause persistent irritation

Long-term exposure is mainly associated with repeated irritation (eyes/upper airways) in dusty operations. Apply exposure controls: enclosed transfer where feasible, LEV at bag stations, and routine cleaning (vacuum methods preferred over dry sweeping).

Production Process & Capacity

Fumaric acid is commonly produced by isomerization of maleic acid (from maleic anhydride) followed by crystallization and drying; bio-based fermentation routes are also expanding.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Prepare maleic anhydride/maleic acid solution; control impurities to support selectivity and crystal quality.
2 Isomerization / Conversion
Convert maleic acid (cis) to fumaric acid (trans) under controlled conditions; manage by-products and color.
3 Crystallization & Purification
Crystallize fumaric acid; wash and dry to meet food/industrial specs (particle size, purity, color).
4 Packaging & Logistics
Package as powder/granules in bags/big-bags; control dust during filling and unloading.
