Hazard Classification

GHS: Warning — H302, H319, H373 (avoid ingestion; protect eyes; minimize prolonged exposure)

Ethylene carbonate (EC) is a high-permittivity carbonate solvent widely used in Li-ion electrolytes. Although it has a relatively high flash point, it can be harmful if swallowed and can cause serious eye irritation. Use chemical goggles, gloves, and good hygiene; avoid aerosols and prolonged contact.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated exposure: potential organ effects (STOT RE, H373) — control exposure and follow SDS

EC is classified with a repeated-exposure hazard statement in some GHS summaries (H373). Implement occupational controls: closed handling where possible, eye/skin PPE, and prompt cleanup of spills. Seek medical advice if symptoms persist.

Production Process & Capacity

Industrial EC is commonly produced by carbonation of ethylene oxide (EO) with CO₂ , followed by purification and deep drying for battery-grade use.
1 Feed & Quality Preparation
Dry, low-impurity EO/CO₂ (or EG/urea) are prepared. Moisture/halides are tightly controlled for battery-grade targets.
2 Catalytic Reaction
EO reacts with CO₂ to form EC in the presence of catalysts (various cations/complexes are used industrially).
3 Purification & Polishing
Unreacted EO/CO₂ and by-products are removed. Distillation/adsorption steps help meet solvent specs (color, acidity, trace contaminants).
4 Battery-Grade Drying & Packaging
Deep drying (very low water) and clean handling are applied. Product is stored/packed to avoid moisture pickup and particulate contamination.
