Hazard Classification

Combustible; dust may form explosive mixtures in air; dust can irritate eyes/airways

Sebacic acid is a low-volatility dicarboxylic acid used in polyamides and plasticizer esters. While the bulk solid is not a volatile solvent, finely dispersed dust can create combustible dust hazards. Control dust at conveying and bagging points (local exhaust, enclosed transfer), prevent dust layers, and keep ignition sources away. Use goggles and a suitable particulate respirator where dust is generated.

Chronic Health Effects

Repeated dust exposure may cause irritation; good hygiene & controls recommended

Long-term risk is mainly associated with repeated particulate exposure during unloading, drying, and packaging. Prefer enclosed conveying, maintain housekeeping (vacuum/ATEX-safe methods where applicable), and apply PPE based on workplace risk assessment.

Production Process & Capacity

Sebacic acid is commonly produced via bio-based cleavage of castor-oil (ricinoleic) derivatives followed by acidification and purification; it is then converted to sebacates or used in polyamides.
1 Feedstock Preparation
Castor oil is hydrolyzed/transesterified to ricinoleic derivatives; impurity/water control supports stable yields.
2 Alkaline Cleavage / Fusion
Ricinoleic derivatives are cleaved under caustic/high-temperature conditions to sebacate salts; process control limits by-products.
3 Acidification & Purification
Sebacate is acidified to sebacic acid; crystallization/filtration and drying achieve product-grade purity.
4 Storage & Logistics
Handled as flakes/powder in bags/bulk; enclosed transfer and dust collection reduce exposure and combustible dust risk.
