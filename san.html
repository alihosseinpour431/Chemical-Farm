Hazard Classification

Combustible thermoplastic; manage heat sources and dust

SAN is typically supplied as pellets/granules. In storage and processing, control ignition sources, prevent dust accumulation, and use local ventilation at extrusion/injection points. During overheating, fumes may form; follow plant HSE procedures.

Chronic Health Considerations

Processing fumes and dust may irritate; ensure ventilation and PPE

For long-term exposure control, focus on ventilation during melting/compounding, housekeeping for dust, and low-residual monomer specifications from suppliers. Use gloves and safety glasses during handling and maintenance.

Production Process & Capacity

SAN is produced by copolymerization of styrene + acrylonitrile (mass/suspension/emulsion) , followed by devolatilization (monomer recovery), extrusion, and pelletizing. Asia-Pacific is the largest demand center— with China as a key driver in packaging, electronics, and appliances .
1Monomer Preparation
Styrene and acrylonitrile are purified, inhibitors controlled, and feeds conditioned for stable copolymer composition and color.
2Copolymerization Reactor
Polymerization (mass/suspension/emulsion) forms SAN with typical 70–80% styrene and 20–30% acrylonitrile, balancing clarity, rigidity, and chemical resistance.
3Devolatilization & Monomer Recovery
Residual monomers are stripped and recovered to meet product specs and reduce odor/VOC; recovered monomers are recycled back to feed preparation.
4Extrusion, Pelletizing & QC
SAN melt is extruded and pelletized; QC focuses on clarity, MFR, residual monomer, and heat resistance. China-focused supply chains often prioritize high-throughput pellet logistics and appliance-grade consistency.
